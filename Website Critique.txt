    <h1>Web Site Critiques</h1>

    <h2>Futurosity</h2>

    <p>
        When I first visited the Futurosity website, something immediately felt suspicious.
        After a closer look, I realized that the issue was the awkward layout, particularly
        the off-center heading. This design choice was the first thing I noticed and was
        quickly off-putting, as it made the site feel unpolished.
    </p>

    <p>
        Overall, the website organizes its information fairly well. Content is easy to
        locate by scrolling up and down the page, and most of the links are positioned on
        the right side. The structure makes sense for a vertically scrolling design, though
        the experience became confusing when several buttons failed to work properly.
    </p>

    <p>
        The media elements on the site enhance the overall appearance and provide strong
        contrast against the plain background. The black and gold color scheme is visually
        appealing, simple, and easy on the eyes. The animated GIF used for the heading was a
        clever idea, and it reminded me of a casino, which fits well with the lottery theme.
        However, the off-center placement made the effect distracting.
    </p>

    <p>
        The website loaded very quickly, taking approximately two seconds to appear after
        clicking the link. This load time is reasonable and comparable to other websites.
        All of the text on the site was readable, with neat, professional fonts and
        appropriate sizing. The plain background made it easy to distinguish all elements.
    </p>

    <p>
        The website did not include a navigation bar, but one was not necessary due to the
        limited amount of content. While there were several links and buttons available,
        none of them functioned correctly. Some had no effect, while others redirected back
        to the homepage.
    </p>

    <p>
        The color scheme of gold, black, and white remained consistent throughout the site
        and reinforced the lottery motif. The site adjusted when viewed in a smaller
        window, allowing the text and images to flow more smoothly. However, two buttons
        disappeared when the window was resized, and the off-center heading became even
        harder to view.
    </p>

    <p>
        Overall, the website was unusual due to its nonfunctional buttons. Although the
        creative use of a GIF heading and lottery-themed colors and symbols was effective,
        the alignment and usability issues ultimately detracted from the user experience.
    </p>
